Waffle House Index

The Waffle House Index is an informal metric used by FEMA and others to gauge the severity of disasters based on the status of Waffle House restaurant locations.

Table of Contents

Origin

The term was popularized by Craig Fugate, former FEMA Administrator, who noted that Waffle House's ability to stay open or close reflects disaster severity.

Usage in Disaster Response

Emergency management officials monitor Waffle House locations to assess disaster impact and response needs. A closed Waffle House is considered a serious indicator of damage.

Color Code System

The index uses three levels:

  • Green: Full menu available, minimal impact.
  • Yellow: Limited menu, supply issues, or power loss.
  • Red: Location closed, severe damage.
