Waffle House Index

The Waffle House Index is an informal metric used by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) to determine the effect of a storm and the likely scale of assistance required for disaster recovery. The term was coined by Craig Fugate, former head of FEMA.

Overview

The index is based on the reputation of Waffle House, a restaurant chain known for its 24-hour, 365-day-a-year service, to remain open during extreme weather events. If a Waffle House restaurant is closed due to a storm, the situation is considered severe.

Categories

  • Green: Full menu available, minimal or no damage.
  • Yellow: Limited menu available, power source is an issue (generator in use), or food supplies are low.
  • Red: Waffle House is closed, indicating severe damage or dangerous conditions.

Use in Disaster Response

FEMA uses the Waffle House Index as one of several measures to assess disaster impact and allocate resources. Because Waffle House has strong emergency protocols, a closure is a sign of serious damage in an area.
